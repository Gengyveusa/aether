## Cancer Amplification

Glutathione (GSH) is the cell's primary thiol antioxidant and the ultimate protector of quantum biological architectures against decoherence-inducing oxidative damage. In cancer:

- **Elevated GSH in resistant tumors**: Chemotherapy-resistant tumors show 2-10x elevated GSH levels. This isn't just drug resistance — elevated GSH creates a *quantum protection shield* that maintains coherence, tunneling, and spin dynamics in the hostile tumor microenvironment.
- **NRF2 constitutive activation**: The master antioxidant transcription factor NRF2 is constitutively active in many cancers (KEAP1 mutations in 15-30% of lung adenocarcinoma). NRF2 drives GCL and GSS expression, flooding the cell with GSH.
- **GSH protects Complex I Fe-S clusters**: GSH chelates iron and reduces superoxide at Complex I, maintaining the precise Fe-S geometry required for quantum electron tunneling. Cancer cells' elevated GSH ensures tunnel integrity even under high metabolic flux.
- **GSH stabilizes CRY radical pairs**: The FAD semiquinone radical in cryptochrome is vulnerable to premature quenching by reactive oxygen species. GSH scavenges peroxyl radicals in the CRY microenvironment, extending radical pair lifetime and coherent spin dynamics.
- **GSH maintains enzyme DAD geometry**: Oxidative modification of active-site cysteines widens donor-acceptor distances in tunneling enzymes. GSH prevents this oxidation, preserving the compressed DAD geometry required for efficient hydrogen tunneling.

## Aging Decoherence

In aging, GSH depletion is arguably THE primary driver of quantum decoherence across all architectures:

- **GSH/GSSG ratio decline**: The GSH/GSSG ratio drops from ~100:1 in young tissue to ~10:1 in aged tissue. This 10-fold shift means oxidative damage proceeds unchecked, degrading every quantum architecture simultaneously.
- **GCL activity decline**: Glutamate-cysteine ligase (the rate-limiting GSH synthesis enzyme) activity decreases 30-50% with age. Cysteine availability also declines.
- **GPX efficiency loss**: Glutathione peroxidase selenium cofactor availability decreases. GPX4 decline specifically leads to lipid peroxidation of cardiolipin, destabilizing ETC supercomplexes.
- **NRF2 signaling impairment**: Age-related NRF2 pathway impairment (reduced nuclear translocation, increased KEAP1 expression) blunts the antioxidant response, creating a negative feedback loop of GSH depletion → oxidative damage → further GSH depletion.
- **Systemic decoherence cascade**: When GSH falls below critical thresholds, ALL four quantum architectures decohere simultaneously — coherence lifetimes shorten (Complex I oxidation), tunneling efficiency drops (enzyme active-site modification), electron leak increases (Fe-S cluster damage), and radical pair lifetimes collapse (Trp oxidation in CRY).
