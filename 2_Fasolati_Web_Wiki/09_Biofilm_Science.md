## Why Biofilm, Not Bacteria

Periodontal disease is not caused by free-floating (planktonic) bacteria. It is caused by **structured microbial communities** -- biofilms -- that create a protected microenvironment where pathogens thrive, resist antibiotics, and generate chronic inflammatory signaling.

> Treating individual bacteria misses the point. You must dismantle the architecture.

---

## Biofilm Architecture

### What a Biofilm Is

| Property | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Definition** | A structured community of microorganisms embedded in a self-produced extracellular matrix attached to a surface |
| **Matrix composition** | Extracellular DNA (eDNA), polysaccharides (PNAG), proteins, lipids |
| **Function** | Physical protection from immune cells, antibiotics, and environmental stress |
| **Antibiotic resistance** | Biofilm bacteria are 100-1,000x more resistant to antibiotics than planktonic counterparts |
| **Communication** | Quorum sensing: bacteria coordinate behavior through chemical signaling molecules |

## Enzymatic Matrix Degradation (Stage 1 Science)

The Lytica BREACH stage ([[03_Lytica_Biofilm_Intervention]]) targets each structural component of the biofilm matrix with a matched enzyme:

| Matrix Component | Enzyme | Mechanism | Result |
|-----------------|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Oxygen gradients** | H2O2 effervescence | Oxidative disruption of internal O2 gradients | Destabilizes anaerobic microenvironments |
| **eDNA scaffold** | DNase I | Enzymatic cleavage of extracellular DNA | Collapses the reinforcing mesh |
| **PNAG polysaccharide** | Dispersin B | Hydrolysis of poly-N-acetylglucosamine | Dismantles the primary structural scaffold |
| **Adhesion proteins** | Proteinase K | Serine protease cleavage of surface proteins | Detaches colonies from surfaces and each other |

**Combined effect:** 4.5 log colony reduction (>99.99%)

### Why Enzymes Over Antibiotics

| Approach | Kills Bacteria? | Destroys Matrix? | Preserves Commensals? | Long-Term Use? |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|----------------------|---------------|
| **Antibiotics** | Yes (indiscriminate) | No | No | No -- resistance |
| **Chlorhexidine** | Yes (indiscriminate) | Partially | No | No -- staining, taste |
| **Enzymatic degradation** | No (exposes them) | **Yes** | **Yes** | **Yes** |

> Enzymes don't kill bacteria -- they strip away the biofilm's protective architecture, exposing the pathogens inside to targeted elimination (Stage 2) or the host's own immune system.

## Bacteriophage Targeting (Stage 2 Science)

The Lytica HUNT stage deploys **species-specific bacteriophages** against keystone periodontal pathogens:

### How Phages Work

| Step | Process |
|------|---------|
| 1 | Phage binds to **species-specific surface receptors** on target bacterium |
| 2 | Phage injects genetic material |
| 3 | Bacterial cell machinery produces new phage particles |
| 4 | Cell lyses (bursts), releasing new phages to target remaining bacteria |

### Ecological Precision

| Property | Antibiotics | Bacteriophages |
|----------|------------|----------------|
| **Target spectrum** | Broad -- kills many species | **Species-specific** -- binds only to target receptors |
| **Commensal impact** | Severe dysbiosis | **None** -- cannot bind commensal receptors |
| **Resistance development** | Rapid (horizontal gene transfer) | Slower (phages co-evolve with hosts) |
| **Self-amplifying** | No -- fixed dose | **Yes** -- phages multiply at infection site |
| **Biofilm penetration** | Poor | Good -- phages carry depolymerases |

### Adjunct Agents

| Agent | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|
| **Recombinant endolysins** | Phage-derived enzymes that degrade peptidoglycan cell wall from the outside -- kills bacteria without needing to infect them |
| **Lactoferrin** | Iron-binding glycoprotein that sequesters iron from pathogen metabolism -- starves iron-dependent species like *P. gingivalis* |

## Ecological Remodeling (Loria Science)

The daily maintenance layer ([[04_Loria_Molecular_Purification]]) prevents biofilm re-establishment through ecological remodeling rather than chemical killing:

| Mechanism | Agent | Effect |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Matrix dispersal** | Biofilm-dispersing enzymes | Prevent polysaccharide scaffold reformation |
| **Surface barrier** | High MW hyaluronic acid | Physical barrier on mucosal surface prevents bacterial adhesion |
| **Tissue repair** | Low MW hyaluronic acid | Stimulates fibroblast proliferation (+35% in 7 days); accelerates re-epithelialization |
| **Selective antimicrobial** | CPC at 0.05% | Low-concentration cetylpyridinium chloride selectively suppresses pathogens while preserving commensal diversity |
| **Sustained contact** | Pullulan + PVP bio-adhesion | Extended mucosal residence time ensures therapeutic effect persists for hours |

## Keystone Pathogen Model

The biofilm is not a uniform community of equally dangerous bacteria. It follows a **keystone pathogen model** where a small number of species -- particularly *P. gingivalis* -- disproportionately drive the dysbiotic shift:

| Species | Role | Significance |
|---------|------|-------------|
| ***P. gingivalis*** | Keystone pathogen | Low abundance but high impact -- produces gingipains that manipulate immune response, enabling the entire community to shift toward dysbiosis |
| ***F. nucleatum*** | Bridge organism | Physically connects early colonizers to late colonizers; enables biofilm maturation |
| ***T. forsythia*** | Red complex member | Proteolytic; associated with severe periodontal destruction |
| ***A. actinomycetemcomitans*** | Aggressive pathogen | Produces leukotoxin; associated with aggressive periodontitis phenotypes |

> This is why Lytica uses targeted phages rather than broad-spectrum killing: eliminate the keystone species, and the dysbiotic community collapses. Eliminate everything, and you create an ecological vacuum that pathogens recolonize first.
