## Stage 1: BREACH -- Matrix Degradation

**Purpose:** Collapse the extracellular biofilm matrix to expose protected pathogens

| Agent | Type | Mechanism |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **H2O2 effervescence** | Oxidative disruption | Disrupts biofilm oxygen gradients |
| **DNase I** | Nuclease enzyme | Degrades extracellular DNA scaffold |
| **Dispersin B** | Glycoside hydrolase | Hydrolyzes PNAG (poly-N-acetylglucosamine) adhesin matrix |
| **Proteinase K** | Serine protease | Cleaves surface-bound adhesion proteins |

**Combined result:** 4.5 log colony reduction (>99.99% biofilm disruption)

### Why Each Agent Matters

- **H2O2 effervescence:** Biofilms maintain internal oxygen gradients that protect anaerobic pathogens. Effervescent H2O2 disrupts these gradients, destabilizing the microenvironment.
- **DNase I:** Extracellular DNA (eDNA) is a critical structural component of biofilm matrix. DNase I enzymatically degrades this scaffold.
- **Dispersin B:** PNAG is a polysaccharide adhesin produced by many biofilm-forming bacteria. Dispersin B specifically hydrolyzes this matrix component.
- **Proteinase K:** Surface adhesion proteins anchor bacteria to each other and to host tissue. Proteinase K cleaves these proteins, detaching colonies.

## Stage 2: HUNT -- Targeted Pathogen Elimination

**Purpose:** Selectively eliminate keystone periodontal pathogens while sparing commensal flora

| Agent | Type | Mechanism |
|-------|------|-----------|
| **Bacteriophages** | Species-specific viruses | Bind exclusively to bacterial surface receptors on target species |
| **Recombinant endolysins** | Phage-derived enzymes | Degrade peptidoglycan cell wall from the outside |
| **Lactoferrin** | Iron-binding glycoprotein | Sequesters iron from pathogen metabolism, starving iron-dependent species |
| **Dual-weight HA carrier** | Hyaluronic acid matrix | Ensures 4+ hours mucosal residence time for sustained therapeutic contact |

### Target Pathogens

| Pathogen | Role | Why Targeted |
|----------|------|-------------|
| ***P. gingivalis*** | Keystone pathogen | Produces gingipains, drives dysbiotic shift, OMV-mediated systemic effects |
| ***T. forsythia*** | Red complex member | Proteolytic; associated with severe periodontitis |
| ***A. actinomycetemcomitans*** | Aggressive periodontitis pathogen | Leukotoxin production, tissue invasion |

### Ecological Precision

> The bacteriophages used in Stage 2 bind **exclusively** to species-specific surface receptors. Unlike broad-spectrum antibiotics, they cannot harm commensal organisms. This is targeted ecological rescue, not scorched earth.
