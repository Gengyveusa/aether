# Competitive Landscape

How [[Spring Leaf Bio]]'s [[4-Signal Construct]] compares to existing periodontal regeneration products.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

| Company | Product | Mechanism | Neural Component | Status |
|---------|---------|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| [[Straumann - Emdogain]] | Emdogain | Enamel matrix derivative | **None** | Market leader |
| [[Geistlich - Bio-Oss]] | Bio-Oss + membrane | Bone graft + membrane | **None** | Established |
| [[BioHorizons - GEM 21S]] | GEM 21S | PDGF-BB + β-TCP | Indirect | Established |
| [[Spring Leaf Bio]] | [[4-Signal Construct]] | 4-signal hydrogel | **Primary driver** | Pre-clinical |

## The Neural Gap

Every existing product ignores the [[Neural-First Paradigm]]:

- **Emdogain** mimics developmental enamel proteins but doesn't address neural signaling
- **Bio-Oss** provides a scaffold for bone but no biological signaling at all
- **GEM 21S** includes [[PDGF-BB]] which has *indirect* neural effects, but doesn't drive neural ingrowth as a primary mechanism

This is why current therapies produce the outcomes documented in [[Clinical Evidence Gap]] — structure without function.

## Spring Leaf's Moat

1. **First-mover advantage** in neural-first periodontal regeneration
2. **Strong IP** on the [[4-Signal Construct]] formulation
3. **Paradigm shift** — competitors would need to fundamentally redesign their products
4. **Clinical differentiation** — proprioception and functional attachment are measurable outcomes
5. **[[Hydrogel Technology]]** optimized specifically for this application

## Competitive Risk

- Incumbents have established distribution and clinical relationships
- Spring Leaf is pre-clinical while competitors are generating revenue
- Mitigation: [[DSO Go-to-Market Strategy]] provides faster adoption path
- Mitigation: [[Regulatory Strategy|510(k) pathway]] provides fastest regulatory route
