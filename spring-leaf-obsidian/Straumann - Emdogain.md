# Straumann — Emdogain

The current **market leader** in periodontal regeneration. A key competitor to [[Spring Leaf Bio]].

## Product Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Company** | Straumann |
| **Product** | Emdogain |
| **Mechanism** | Enamel matrix derivative (EMD) |
| **Neural component** | **None** |
| **Market position** | Market leader |

## How It Works

Emdogain contains enamel matrix proteins (primarily amelogenins) that mimic developmental signals:
- Applied to root surface during periodontal surgery
- Promotes formation of acellular cementum
- Stimulates periodontal ligament cell activity
- Aims to recapitulate developmental processes

## Limitations

Despite market leadership, Emdogain has significant limitations documented in [[Clinical Evidence Gap]]:

- **No neural component** — ignores the [[Neural-First Paradigm]] entirely
- Produces cementum-like tissue but inconsistent PDL regeneration
- Outcomes often include long junctional epithelium rather than true attachment
- Variable clinical results
- Contributes to the 87% incomplete regeneration rate

## As a 510(k) Predicate

Emdogain serves as a **predicate device** for [[Spring Leaf Bio]]'s [[Regulatory Strategy|510(k)/De Novo pathway]]:
- Established regulatory precedent for growth-factor-based periodontal products
- Similar intended use (periodontal regeneration)
- Spring Leaf's [[4-Signal Construct]] offers a superior mechanism of action
