# BMP-7 — Bone Morphogenetic Protein 7

Signal #3 in the [[4-Signal Construct]]. Drives periodontal ligament (PDL) fiber differentiation after neural and vascular networks are established.

## Role in the Construct

- **Function:** PDL differentiation — directs progenitor cells toward functional ligament
- **Phase:** PDL (third phase of the [[Neural-First Paradigm]] cascade)
- **Mechanism:** Osteogenic and cementogenic differentiation, Sharpey's fiber formation
- **Criticality:** Without BMP-7, you get bone fill but not functional PDL attachment

## Biological Function

BMP-7 (also known as OP-1) is a member of the TGF-β superfamily:
- Induces osteoblast and cementoblast differentiation
- Promotes periodontal ligament fiber organization
- Drives formation of functional Sharpey's fibers
- Prevents ankylosis by maintaining PDL space

## Cascade Position

```
[[NGF]] → Neural ingrowth
   ↓
[[PDGF-BB]] → Vascular network
   ↓
[BMP-7] → PDL differentiation
   ↓
[[BDNF]] → Neural maintenance
```

## Why BMP-7 and Not Other BMPs?

- BMP-2 tends toward bone formation (ankylosis risk)
- BMP-7 has a stronger PDL/cementum differentiation profile
- Better safety profile in periodontal applications
- Supports functional attachment rather than fusion
