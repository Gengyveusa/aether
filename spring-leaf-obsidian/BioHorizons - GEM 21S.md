# BioHorizons — GEM 21S

A growth-factor-based periodontal regeneration product. The closest existing competitor to [[Spring Leaf Bio]]'s approach.

## Product Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Company** | BioHorizons |
| **Product** | GEM 21S |
| **Mechanism** | [[PDGF-BB]] + β-TCP (beta-tricalcium phosphate) |
| **Neural component** | **Indirect** (via PDGF-BB) |
| **Market position** | Established |

## How It Works

GEM 21S combines a growth factor with a bone scaffold:
- **[[PDGF-BB]]** stimulates angiogenesis and cell recruitment
- **β-TCP** provides osteoconductive scaffold
- Applied surgically to periodontal defects
- PDGF-BB has known indirect neurotrophic properties

## The "Indirect Neural" Effect

GEM 21S is the only current product with *any* neural relevance:
- [[PDGF-BB]] has documented neurotrophic properties
- However, neural effects are **indirect** and **not the primary mechanism**
- Product is designed and marketed for bone/vascular regeneration
- No [[NGF]], [[BDNF]], or [[BMP-7]] — missing 3 of 4 signals in [[4-Signal Construct]]

This partial validation supports [[Spring Leaf Bio]]'s thesis while highlighting the need for a complete [[Neural-First Paradigm]] approach.

## As a 510(k) Predicate

GEM 21S serves as a **predicate device** for [[Spring Leaf Bio]]'s [[Regulatory Strategy|510(k)/De Novo pathway]]:
- Established precedent for growth-factor + scaffold periodontal products
- Similar delivery concept (biologic + carrier matrix)
- Demonstrates FDA comfort with growth-factor-based periodontal products

## Competitive Position vs. Spring Leaf

| Dimension | GEM 21S | Spring Leaf |

| Neural approach | Indirect | Primary driver |
