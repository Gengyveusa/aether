# Neural-First Paradigm

The core scientific insight behind [[Spring Leaf Bio]]'s platform: **neural ingrowth precedes and coordinates periodontal regeneration**.

## The Developmental Hierarchy

```
NERVES (neural ingrowth initiates cascade)
   ↓
VESSELS (vascular network follows neural pathways)
   ↓
PDL (functional periodontal ligament)
```

This hierarchy is preserved in adult regeneration but **ignored by all current therapies** — which is why they fail (see [[Clinical Evidence Gap]]).

## Why Neural-First Matters

Current approaches focus on bone/tissue scaffolds alone and ignore the neural component entirely. The result:

- **Ankylosis** instead of functional attachment
- **No proprioception** — patients can't sense bite force
- **Long junctional epithelium** instead of true connective tissue attachment
- **87% incomplete regeneration** rates
- **30–50% recurrent disease** within 5 years

## The Insight

During embryonic development, neural crest cells direct periodontal tissue formation. The same signaling hierarchy exists in adult tissue but requires the right molecular cues in the right sequence.

[[Spring Leaf Bio]] recapitulates this with the [[4-Signal Construct]]:

1. **[[NGF]]** — initiates neural ingrowth (Phase 1: NERVES)
2. **[[PDGF-BB]]** — supports vascular network along neural pathways (Phase 2: VESSELS)
3. **[[BMP-7]]** — drives PDL fiber differentiation (Phase 3: PDL)
4. **[[BDNF]]** — sustains neural survival throughout the process
