# Geistlich — Bio-Oss

An established bone graft + membrane system for periodontal regeneration. A key competitor to [[Spring Leaf Bio]].

## Product Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Company** | Geistlich |
| **Product** | Bio-Oss (+ Bio-Gide membrane) |
| **Mechanism** | Bone graft + collagen membrane |
| **Neural component** | **None** |
| **Market position** | Established |

## How It Works

Bio-Oss is a guided tissue regeneration (GTR) approach:
- Bovine-derived hydroxyapatite bone graft placed in defect
- Bio-Gide collagen membrane placed over graft
- Membrane excludes epithelium, allows bone/PDL regeneration
- Scaffold-based approach — provides structure for cell ingrowth

## Limitations

- **No biological signaling** — purely structural scaffold
- **No neural component** — completely ignores [[Neural-First Paradigm]]
- Relies on host cell migration and endogenous signaling
- Inconsistent PDL regeneration
- Primarily produces bone fill, not functional attachment
- Contributes to the [[Clinical Evidence Gap|87% incomplete regeneration rate]]

## Competitive Position vs. Spring Leaf

| Dimension | Bio-Oss | Spring Leaf |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Approach | Scaffold (structural) | Signaling (biological) |
| Neural | None | Primary driver |
| Active agents | None | [[4-Signal Construct]] |
| Delivery | Surgical placement | [[Hydrogel Technology\|Injectable]] |
| Outcome | Bone fill | Functional PDL |

## Different Market Segment

Bio-Oss primarily targets bone defects and ridge augmentation — partially overlapping with but distinct from [[Spring Leaf Bio]]'s focus on functional periodontal regeneration with proprioception.
