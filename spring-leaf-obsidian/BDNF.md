# BDNF — Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor

Signal #4 in the [[4-Signal Construct]]. Ensures the survival of newly formed neural tissue throughout the regeneration process.

## Role in the Construct

- **Function:** Neural survival — maintains and matures neural networks
- **Phase:** Maintenance (operates throughout the [[Neural-First Paradigm]] cascade)
- **Mechanism:** Prevents apoptosis of new neurons, promotes synaptic maturation
- **Criticality:** Without BDNF, NGF-initiated neural growth regresses

## Biological Function

BDNF is the most abundant neurotrophic factor in the CNS and PNS:
- Promotes neuronal survival and differentiation
- Enhances synaptic plasticity
- Supports long-term maintenance of neural circuits
- Complements [[NGF]]'s growth-initiating role with survival signaling

## Cascade Position

```
[[NGF]] → Neural ingrowth (initiation)
   ↓
[[PDGF-BB]] → Vascular network
   ↓
[[BMP-7]] → PDL differentiation
   ↓
[BDNF] → Neural maintenance (survival)
```

## NGF + BDNF: The Neural Pair

The [[4-Signal Construct]] includes two neurotrophic factors:
- **[[NGF]]** — initiates neural ingrowth (growth signal)
- **BDNF** — sustains neural tissue (survival signal)

Together they ensure that neural networks don't just form but **persist** — which is essential for proprioception and functional periodontal regeneration.
