# Three-Phase Commercialization

[[Spring Leaf Bio]]'s staged approach to market, progressively increasing product complexity and margin.

## Overview

| Phase | Product | Regulatory | Timeline | Margin |
|-------|---------|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Phase 1** | Cell-free construct | 510(k)/De Novo (CDRH) | 18–24 months | 79% |
| **Phase 2** | Construct + cells | BLA (CBER) | 36–48 months | 73% |
| **Phase 3** | Vertical integration | BLA Supplement (CBER) | 48–60 months | 82% |

## Phase 1: Cell-Free Construct

**The [[4-Signal Construct]] in [[Hydrogel Technology|HA/alginate hydrogel]]**

- Hydrogel + 4 growth factors ([[NGF]], [[PDGF-BB]], [[BMP-7]], [[BDNF]])
- No cellular component — simplifies [[Regulatory Strategy|regulatory path]]
- [[Manufacturing Plan|CMO manufactured]], $180 COGS
- ASP $850, **79% gross margin**
- [[DSO Go-to-Market Strategy|DSO-first]] commercial strategy

**Why start here:**
- Fastest regulatory path (510(k)/De Novo)
- Generates revenue to fund Phase 2
- Builds clinical evidence base
- Establishes [[Manufacturing Plan|manufacturing]] and [[Quality Systems]]

## Phase 2: Construct + Cells

**Adding autologous or allogeneic cells to the construct**

- Enhanced regenerative potential
- Requires BLA through CBER
- RMAT designation eligible — see [[Regulatory Strategy]]
- [[Manufacturing Plan|Hybrid manufacturing]], $650 COGS
- ASP $2,400, **73% gross margin**

## Phase 3: Vertical Integration

**Full in-house cell sourcing and manufacturing**

- Complete control over production
- [[Manufacturing Plan|In-house manufacturing]], $420 COGS (down from $650)
- Same ASP $2,400, **82% gross margin**
- 500,000 units/year capacity
- [[Supply Chain|In-house growth factor production]]
