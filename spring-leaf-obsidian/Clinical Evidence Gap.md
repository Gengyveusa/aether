# Clinical Evidence Gap

The fundamental problem that [[Spring Leaf Bio]] exists to solve: **current periodontal regeneration produces structure without function**.

## The Numbers

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Incomplete regeneration rate** | 87% |
| **Common outcome** | Long junctional epithelium (not true attachment) |
| **Recurrent disease** | 30–50% within 5 years |

## Why Current Approaches Fail

Existing products focus on bone and tissue scaffolds while **ignoring the neural component**:

| Problem | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Scaffold-only thinking** | Focus on bone/tissue structure, not function |
| **No neural component** | [[Neural-First Paradigm]] completely ignored |
| **Ankylosis risk** | Bone fuses to root without PDL space |
| **No proprioception** | Patients can't sense bite force on regenerated tissue |
| **Wrong tissue type** | Long junctional epithelium instead of connective tissue attachment |

## Competitor Outcomes

| Product | What It Produces | What's Missing |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| [[Straumann - Emdogain]] | Variable cementum, inconsistent PDL | Neural signaling |
| [[Geistlich - Bio-Oss]] | Bone fill, scaffold structure | All biological signaling |
| [[BioHorizons - GEM 21S]] | Bone + vascular response | Direct neural, PDL differentiation |

## The Insight

The developmental biology is clear: **neural ingrowth precedes and coordinates periodontal regeneration**. This hierarchy (NERVES → VESSELS → PDL) is preserved in adult tissue but no current therapy leverages it.

See [[Neural-First Paradigm]] for the full scientific rationale.
