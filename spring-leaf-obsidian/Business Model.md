# Business Model

[[Spring Leaf Bio]]'s unit economics and revenue model across the [[Three-Phase Commercialization]] strategy.

## Unit Economics by Phase

| Metric | Phase 1 | Phase 2 | Phase 3 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| **ASP** | $850 | $2,400 | $2,400 |
| **COGS** | $180 | $650 | $420 |
| **Gross Margin** | **79%** | **73%** | **82%** |
| Product | Cell-free construct | Construct + cells | Vertically integrated |

### COGS Progression

See [[Manufacturing Plan]] for details on cost reduction:

- Phase 1 → Phase 2: $180 → $140 (**-22%** on hydrogel alone, cells add $510)
- Phase 2 → Phase 3: $650 → $420 (**-35%** via vertical integration)

## Revenue Streams

1. **Direct sales** to DSOs and periodontists
2. **Distributor partnerships** for broader reach
3. **Training programs** for clinical adoption
4. **Premium pricing** justified by superior clinical outcomes

## Go-to-Market

Primary channel: [[DSO Go-to-Market Strategy]]

- **$340M** DSO beachhead within $1.1B US addressable market
- Centralized purchasing → faster sales cycles
- Standardized protocols → easier clinical integration
- Data-driven outcomes → evidence-based adoption

## Pricing Strategy

- **Phase 1 ($850):** Positioned as premium to [[Straumann - Emdogain|Emdogain]] and [[BioHorizons - GEM 21S|GEM 21S]], justified by neural-first clinical differentiation
- **Phase 2 ($2,400):** Reflects cell-based therapy premium; comparable to advanced biologics
- **Phase 3 ($2,400):** Same ASP, dramatically improved margins via [[Manufacturing Plan|vertical integration]]

## Path to Revenue

1. [[Regulatory Strategy|510(k) clearance]] (Month 24)
2. DSO pilot partnerships (LOIs by Month 18)
3. Commercial launch through DSO channel
4. Distributor expansion
5. Phase 2 premium product introduction
