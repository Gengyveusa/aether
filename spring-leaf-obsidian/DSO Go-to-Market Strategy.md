# DSO Go-to-Market Strategy

[[Spring Leaf Bio]]'s beachhead strategy targeting Dental Service Organizations (DSOs) as the primary commercial channel.

## Why DSOs First?

The $340M DSO segment within the [[Market Opportunity|$1.1B US addressable market]] offers:

| Advantage | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Centralized purchasing** | Single decision-maker for dozens/hundreds of locations |
| **Standard protocols** | Easier clinical integration across all sites |
| **Data-driven outcomes** | Track and measure regeneration results systematically |
| **Volume pricing** | Predictable revenue with contracted volumes |
| **Faster adoption** | Corporate decision → immediate rollout |
| **Reference sites** | Successful DSOs become proof points for broader market |

## Market Sizing

- **Global periodontal regeneration:** $4.2B
- **US addressable:** $1.1B
- **DSO beachhead:** **$340M**

## Execution Plan

### Phase 1: LOIs (Month 0–18)
- Identify top 10 target DSO partners
- Present [[Neural-First Paradigm]] clinical differentiation
- Negotiate pilot program terms
- Secure letters of intent by Month 18 (see [[Funding|18-Month Milestones]])

### Phase 2: Pilot Programs (Post-510(k) Clearance)
- Deploy [[4-Signal Construct]] at 3–5 DSO pilot sites
- Collect outcome data (functional attachment, proprioception)
- Refine clinical protocols
- Build evidence base for broader rollout

### Phase 3: Scale
- Expand to contracted DSO networks
- Add distributor partnerships for independent periodontists
- Launch training programs
- Establish premium pricing based on documented outcomes

## Why DSOs Over Independent Practices?

| Factor | DSOs | Independent |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Decision speed | Fast (centralized) | Slow (individual) |
| Sales cycle | 1 buyer = many sites | 1 buyer = 1 site |
| Protocol adoption | Standardized | Variable |
| Data collection | Systematic | Ad hoc |
| Volume commitment | Contractual | Uncertain |
| Reference value | High (branded network) | Low (single practice) |
