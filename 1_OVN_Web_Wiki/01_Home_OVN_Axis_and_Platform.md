## Hero Statement

**"The Mouth-Body Connection Is the Next Frontier of Medicine."**

Oral microbes continuously release inflammatory and signaling molecules that travel through blood vessels and nerve pathways, influencing the heart, brain, and immune system. The **OVN Axis** (Oral-Vascular-Neural) provides a structured scientific framework to investigate this oral–vascular–neural signaling interface.

### OVN Axis Diagram

The conceptual model traces a directional pathway:

```
Mouth → Bloodstream → Heart / Brain / Immune System
```

Periodontal pathogens and their byproducts enter circulation through inflamed gingival tissue, creating a systemic inflammatory burden that can reach distant organ systems.

## Why Periodontal Health Matters Systemically

Chronic inflammation originating in the periodontium is increasingly viewed as a modifiable upstream contributor to major systemic conditions. The global scale is striking:

| Condition | Statistic | Context |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Cardiovascular disease | ~32% of all deaths worldwide | Leading global cause of mortality |
| Cancer | ~1 in 6 cancer deaths | Pathways under exploration for possible oral microbial involvement |
| Dementia | ~1 billion projected by 2050 | Neuro-inflammatory mechanisms under active research |

**Important framing note**: These figures illustrate the enormous potential public-health impact; they do **not** establish periodontitis as a proven direct cause of any specific systemic disease. Periodontal status is best understood as a potentially modifiable risk signal rather than a confirmed causal driver.

## Evidence Tiers (Epistemic Honesty)

The platform uses a three-tier system to communicate the strength of current evidence honestly:

### Tier 1 — Established

Periodontitis produces measurable systemic inflammatory burden. Multiple meta-analyses confirm strong associations with atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD). Periodontal treatment has been linked to improvements in surrogate cardiometabolic markers such as hsCRP and IL-6.

### Tier 2 — Supported

Outer membrane vesicles (OMVs) from key oral pathogens such as *Porphyromonas gingivalis* carry concentrated virulence factors including gingipains, LPS, and fimbriae. These nanoscale packets (20–250 nm) can cross epithelial and endothelial barriers and trigger endothelial activation plus localized inflammation in laboratory models.

### Tier 3 — Hypothesis Under Test

A conserved cellular reprogramming cascade driven by OMVs — involving mitochondrial dysfunction, phenotypic plasticity, and secondary extracellular-vesicle (EV) amplification — is hypothesized to contribute to vascular, neurodegenerative, and oncologic tissue changes. This program is currently being probed in ongoing research.

See [[04_Science_OVN_Axis_and_OMVs]] for the full research presentation and detailed cascade mechanisms.

## OMV Biology & the 5-Step Cascade

Outer membrane vesicles are tiny (20–250 nm) lipid-bilayer particles shed continuously by gram-negative oral bacteria. Unlike free-floating bacteria, OMVs can cross tissue barriers, evade immune detection, and deliver targeted cargo directly to distant host cells.

### Five-Step Sequence

| Step | Name | Mechanism |
|------|------|-----------|
| 1 | **Barrier Disruption** | OMVs penetrate the periodontal epithelium and enter systemic circulation |
| 2 | **Mitochondrial Dysfunction** | Virulence cargo (gingipains, LPS) impairs mitochondrial energy production in target cells |
| 3 | **Phenotypic Reprogramming** | Host cells adopt a pro-inflammatory, pro-remodeling state |
| 4 | **Secondary EV Signals** | Reprogrammed cells release their own signaling vesicles, amplifying the inflammatory message |
| 5 | **Tissue Endpoints** | Cumulative effects manifest in vascular, neural, and neoplastic compartments |

This cascade is the central mechanistic model of the OVN Axis framework. Full detail in [[04_Science_OVN_Axis_and_OMVs]].

## Clinical Action — "What You Can Do Today"

### Do Now (3 actions)

1. **Treat periodontal disease as a systemic health concern**, not merely a local dental issue.
2. **Record periodontal findings routinely** in cardiometabolic and neurologic patient histories.
3. **Set realistic expectations** around biomarker improvements (hsCRP, IL-6) following periodontal therapy.

### Do Not Claim (1 guardrail)

- **Do not** present periodontal disease as a validated causal driver of ASCVD, cancer, or Alzheimer's. The causal evidence is not yet at that level. See [[14_Legal_and_Disclaimers]].
