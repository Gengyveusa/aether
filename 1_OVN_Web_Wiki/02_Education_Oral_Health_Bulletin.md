## Series 1: Diabetes & Oral Health

**Theme**: Bidirectional links between metabolic control, saliva chemistry, and caries/periodontal risk.

| Module | Title | Summary |
|--------|-------|---------|
| 45 | Diabetes, Saliva, and Enamel Risk | Explores how glycemic control alters salivary buffering capacity and enamel demineralization rates. Covers HbA1c correlations with salivary glucose, pH shifts, and caries incidence in diabetic populations. |

**Key concepts**: Salivary glucose elevation → pH drop → demineralization acceleration → caries risk amplification. Bidirectional relationship: periodontal inflammation worsens glycemic control, poor glycemic control worsens periodontal disease.

Tags: #Diabetes #Saliva #Caries-risk #Bidirectional

**Theme**: OMV-mediated pathways connecting oral microbes to distant vascular and neural effects.

## Series 3: Information Collapse

**Theme**: Information-theory framing of dysbiosis and loss of microbial homeostasis.

| Module | Title | Summary |
|--------|-------|---------|
| 48 | Information Collapse in Microbial Ecosystems | Introduces Shannon entropy and ecological information metrics applied to the oral microbiome. Defines "information collapse" as the measurable loss of microbial diversity preceding clinical disease. |
| 49 | Dysbiosis as Signal Degradation | Frames the shift from symbiosis to dysbiosis as a degradation of inter-species communication signals within biofilm communities. |
| 50 | Tipping Points in Oral Ecology | Explores nonlinear dynamics in microbiome transitions — how small perturbations can trigger catastrophic shifts in community composition. |
| 51 | Restoring Microbial Information | Discusses therapeutic approaches aimed at rebuilding microbial diversity and restoring homeostatic signaling — probiotics, targeted antimicrobials, ecological interventions. |

**Key concepts**: Shannon entropy, ecological collapse, tipping-point dynamics, information-theoretic modeling of dysbiosis. This series represents a novel theoretical framing unique to the OVN Nexus platform.

Tags: #Information-Theory #Dysbiosis #Microbiome #Ecology

## Series 4: Biofilm Architecture & Disease

**Theme**: Biofilms as dynamic information-processing systems that drive pathology.

| Module | Title | Summary |
|--------|-------|---------|
| 52 | Biofilm as a Living Architecture | Describes the three-dimensional structure of oral biofilms — EPS matrix, water channels, nutrient gradients, and spatial organization of species. |
| 53 | Quorum Sensing and Biofilm Intelligence | Covers autoinducer-mediated communication within biofilms, collective decision-making, and how biofilm "intelligence" coordinates virulence factor expression. |
| 54 | Biofilm Resistance Mechanisms | Examines why biofilms resist antibiotics (diffusion barriers, persister cells, horizontal gene transfer within the matrix) and immune evasion strategies. |
| 55 | Biofilm Disruption Strategies | Reviews current and experimental approaches to biofilm disruption — mechanical debridement, enzymatic degradation of EPS, quorum-sensing inhibitors, and photodynamic therapy. |

**Key concepts**: EPS matrix architecture, quorum sensing, persister cells, horizontal gene transfer, therapeutic disruption strategies.

Tags: #Biofilm #QuorumSensing #Resistance #Therapeutics
